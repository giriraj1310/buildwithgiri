# How I Went from Gujarat to Ottawa with a Backpack and Landed 3 IBM Internships

Going to Canada was never a conscious decision.

I applied almost on a whim. A friend of mine was so confident about his application that I thought — if he's that sure, and I scored higher than him, why not me? There was no grand plan, no vision board, no five-year roadmap. Just a casual "why not" that changed everything.

Back in India, things weren't looking great. My JEE Mains and VIT entrance exam scores were below average. The traditional paths felt closed. So when Canada said yes, I said yes back.

I landed in Toronto on August 23, 2016, and started my program at Sheridan College with nothing but a backpack, a little money, and zero network.

---

## The Grind Nobody Talks About

Finding internships was brutal.

I watched friends get offers and acceptances one by one. Interviews, callbacks, excitement — for everyone else. For me, silence. Week after week of applying and hearing nothing back.

So I built a system.

Every single morning I would wake up at 5 AM and apply to every job posted on our internal student board until 9 AM. I tailored my resume for each role. I wrote a custom cover letter for every application. No copy-paste. No shortcuts. I did this for about a month straight — like a robot, even when the hope was gone.

And honestly? At some point, I had lost hope. I was going through the motions, applying not because I believed something would come through, but because stopping felt worse than continuing.

Then IBM called.

---

## The One Call That Changed Everything

That was the only interview call I ever received. One call from hundreds of applications.

My future manager was an hour late to the interview.

I didn't move. I waited. I wasn't going anywhere without that call.

When he finally joined, I didn't show frustration or nervousness. I showed up as someone eager to learn, passionate about the work, and full of energy. Because here's what I've learned about internships — they're not really looking for the most technically brilliant person in the room. They're looking for someone who's hungry, enthusiastic, and willing to grow. That's what I showed that day.

I got the offer.

The funny thing? The call came in late November 2017. The co-op didn't start until January 2018. I had to sit with that excitement for weeks.

---

## How One Internship Became Three

My first IBM co-op ran from January to April 2018. I worked hard, showed results, and more importantly — I invested in the relationship with my manager.

I didn't just do good work and disappear. I stayed in touch. I texted him. I asked him directly if he'd be willing to bring me back. That directness led to two more co-ops:

- **September – December 2018** at IBM
- **May – August 2019** at IBM

And then in January 2020, he hired me full time.

Three internships and a full-time offer — all from one relationship I chose to nurture.

---

## What I Want You to Take From This

I didn't get into IBM through a referral. I didn't have connections. I didn't have a perfect GPA or a prestigious degree from India.

I got in because I refused to stop, I showed up even when hope was gone, and I treated one relationship like it mattered — because it did.

Canada taught me that not everything runs on referrals. Sometimes you create your own luck by simply not quitting when everyone else would have.

If you're an international student grinding right now, waking up early, tailoring resumes nobody seems to read — I see you. Keep going. Your one call is coming.
